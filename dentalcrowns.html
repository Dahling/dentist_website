Dental Crowns and Bridges

Crowns

What is a dental crown? A dental crown is a tooth-shaped shell that covers the entire visible portion of the tooth, at and above the gum line. Crowns restore the shape, size, color, and strength of a tooth. They can be made of gold, porcelain, zirconia, or other tooth-colored materials.

Crowns are used to:

Strengthen a tooth with a large filling

Protect a weak tooth from breaking

Restore a tooth that has had a root canal

Attach bridges

Restore a broken tooth

Cover a tooth that is discolored or badly shaped

Cover an implant

The Process of Crowning a Tooth

Preparing a tooth and placing a crown or dental crown procedure usually takes two dental visits. The first visit is for the exam and preparation of the tooth, and the second visit is to place the dental crown on the tooth.

First Visit

A dental exam to check on the health of the tooth and its root, as well as the integrity of surrounding structures

Numb the tooth and gums to minimize discomfort

Recontour top and sides of the tooth that will receive the crown

Take an impression of the reshaped tooth

Impression is sent to a lab that makes the crown, so it will fit perfectly

A temporary crown is placed over the prepared tooth to protect it

Second Visit

The temporary crown is removed

The permanent crown is temporarily placed to check for fit and color

The tooth is numbed

The permanent crown is cemented into place

Bridges

If you are having difficulty chewing or speaking because of missing teeth, a dental bridge of replacement teeth can fill the gap where your teeth used to be. Bridges can be made of gold, alloys, porcelain, or a combination of materials and are usually attached to surrounding teeth for support. The bridge has one or more artificial teeth, called pontics, which are supported by crowned teeth or implants, called abutment teeth.

Three kinds of bridges are:

Removable Bridge or Partial Dentures

Can be removed for sleep or cleaning

Fixed Bridge or Fixed Partial Denture

Can only be removed by your dentist

Implant Bridge

Attaches artificial teeth directly into the jaw permanently

The Dental Bridge Process

Getting a dental bridge usually takes two or more dental visits

First Visit

Abutment teeth are prepared

Re-contoured by removing some enamel to make room for the crowns to be placed

Impressions are made of the teeth (and the gap between the teeth)

Bridge, pontic, and crowns are made by a dental lab using the impressions

A temporary bridge is made to wear until the next appointment

Second Visit

Temporary bridge is removed

New bridge is checked and adjusted for proper fit

If it is a fixed bridge, it will be temporarily placed to make sure it fits correctly

Follow-up Visits

Bridge is checked to make sure it is working properly

If it is a fixed bridge, it is cemented permanently onto the abutment teeth
