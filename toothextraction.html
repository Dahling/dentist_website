Tooth Removal and Extractions


Even though keeping your adult teeth for a lifetime is the ideal, there are times when removal of a tooth, or an extraction, is necessary. We are equipped to perform individual and full mouth teeth extractions in our office. We handle both non-surgical and surgical extractions, including third molars (wisdom teeth).

As part of your consultation, you will be told exactly what to expect. Nobody looks forward to getting a tooth pulled, but we will take care of you in a very gentle manner, and we have sedation options available. It is very important to follow the tooth extraction aftercare instructions to make sure you heal properly and do not have any complications.

Reasons for Extractions

Decay or Trauma

If a tooth is too decayed or broken to be saved with a filling or root canal, or restored with a crown, it will need to be removed.

Gum Disease

When gum disease loosens or significantly damages a tooth, it will need to be pulled or removed surgically to prevent infection or damage to surrounding tissues, including your other teeth.

Crowding

Sometimes the mouth gets crowded with too many teeth, and it causes alignment issues. Another reason for extracting a tooth due to crowding is to prepare for orthodontic treatment.

Infection or Risk of Infection

If tooth decay gets to the pulp, then bacteria can invade the tooth and cause serious infection. When the infection is not caught in time with a root canal, the infection can spread. In that case, the tooth should be extracted.

If your immune system is already compromised due to a medical condition, a tooth may need to be pulled. Even if there is only a slight risk of infection, we want to protect the other parts of the body.

Preparation for Implants or Dentures

When your teeth are not healthy, or you are missing teeth, the remaining teeth may be extracted. This can be to prepare for dental implants, implant-supported dentures, or removable dentures. Depending on the procedure, there may be a need for healing time before replacement teeth are put in.

An Extraction: What to Expect

First, we will numb the area where the problem tooth is. We will make you comfortable with sedation, if needed. We will either pull the tooth out or surgically extract it. Depending on the procedure, you may need a few sutures. Gauze is applied over the wound to stop any bleeding. We will send you home with specific instructions for after care to make sure you heal properly.

After an Extraction

It is important to follow your doctor’s instructions carefully after an extraction.

Recommendations:

  • Take prescribed or recommended painkillers
  • Apply a cold compress to the outside of your mouth to reduce pain and swelling
  • Relax and avoid any strenuous activity
  • Refrain from laying flat – prop your head up with pillows
  • Refrain from using a straw or do anything that requires a sucking motion
  • Change gauze pads regularly
  • Gently rinse your mouth with warm salt water several times a day (after 24 hours)
  • Eat soft foods
  • Carefully brush your teeth and tongue
  • Abstain from smoking
  • Follow up with us!
