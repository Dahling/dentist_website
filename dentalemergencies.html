Dental Emergencies

You should never ignore dental pain. Pain is an indication that something is wrong. Ignoring it could lead to bigger problems which can make the pain even worse. We know emergencies do not wait for appointment times. We will see you right away if you are experiencing any dental pain or emergency dental.

Four Common Dental Emergencies

Toothaches

Symptoms of a toothache include: pain when pressure is applied, extreme sensitivity to hot or cold, or pain when chewing. There may be swelling around the tooth. Toothache usually occurs due to the inflammation of the pulp in the center of the tooth, which is full of nerve endings that can become sensitive.

The swelling and pain can be caused by tooth decay, infection or injury. Toothaches can also be the result of pain radiating from other parts of the body to the jaw. Regardless of the cause of toothache pain, you need to see us right away for urgent dental care.

Infections/Abscesses

A dental abscess is an infection of the mouth, throat, jaw, or face. An abscess is usually the result of tooth decay or gum disease that has been left untreated and spread to surrounding tissues. The symptoms of an abscess are: pain, swelling, and redness of the mouth and face.

Discomfort and pain will build up at the site of the infection. Infections are nothing to mess around with and can spread quickly. An abscess can even cause swelling of the throat and threaten to block the airway. If you have any pain or symptoms of an infection, it is an emergency. Call us immediately.

Injuries

Tooth and mouth injuries are very common, but they can be traumatic. Most of the time a dental injury affects one or more front teeth. In small children, baby teeth are broken or knocked out when learning how to walk. For older children and adults, sports and other physical activities are the main reasons for injuries.

If a tooth is knocked out or broken, be sure to bring the tooth (or the pieces) with you to your appointment. Rinse them in warm water, being careful not to touch the root, then place them in a small container of milk or saliva. Use gauze to apply pressure if there is bleeding. Call us, and we will see you right away for dental emergency care. If our office is not open or you cannot reach us, you may go to the emergency room.

Lost Fillings or Restorations

Having a filling fall out or breaking a crown is not just an inconvenience. Teeth that have fillings or other restorations have had the protective enamel removed. They can be very vulnerable to bacteria in your mouth and be susceptible to infection.

Your affected tooth needs to be cleaned thoroughly and have the filling or crown replaced right away. Please call us if you have any damage to restorations or dental appliances. We will see you as soon as possible.
